Everyone has an estate

Closing an estate is a frustrating experience. If all the puzzle pieces that comprise an estate are not together it will take more time, which could lead to heirs carrying costs and paying more in taxes. This tool will be helpful in assisting the executor or power of attorney of your estate. Organizing your estate will bring peace of mind to you and to your heirs.

Other Considerations

Probate Fees - At death the executor will most likely have to have a court declare your will is valid and in order to deal with certain assets. Depending on the amount of the assets, banks, trust companies and other investment companies will require to at least having a notarized copy of the will or probated prior to releasing the funds. Most provinces will charge probate fees. If you plan your affairs so that your assets pass directly to your heirs the costs of probate fees could be reduced or eliminated. However careful consideration should be given when planning for probate and you must do your due diligence.

Life Insurance or Segregated Funds - Review your insurance policies to make sure that you have enough insurance to protect your family in case of an untimely death, last expenses and tax implications. Also review the beneficiary designations for all policies to confirm your wishes.
